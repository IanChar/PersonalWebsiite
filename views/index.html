About Me

Me

I was born and raised in Lakewood, Colorado and am currently attending University of Colorado at Boulder to study Computer Science and Applied Mathematics (Class of 2017). Aside from computer science and math, I am very passionate about music. I have been playing music since the age of 6 and during that time have participated in several bands playing several instruments such as the drums, clarinet, and piano. I also love to play tennis and was on the varsity team for three of my four years in high school.

Academics

Projects


Expenshare

Expenshare

Expenshare was a school project that I worked on for Software Methods and Tools with Brennan McConnell and Taylor Andrews. The website is an easy way for groups of people to keep track of expenses and how much they owe each other. The website was built with the Django framework and the creation process emphasized the use of Agile ideology. You can see the website here.

Soundscape

Soundscape

Soundscape was an independent project that I collaborated on with Tommy Fan. The website was built with the Django framework and allows users to share songs they like and created on SoundCloud geographically through the Google Maps API. You can see the website here.

MCM

Mathematical Contest in Modeling

The Mathematical Contest in Modeling(MCM) is an international competition held every year by the ACM. I, along with Denis Kazakov and Derek Driggs, represented one of seven CU teams for 2015. The problem we picked to model was the best way to distribute a hypothetical vaccine for ebola. We did this by representing the spread of disease with a Markov network. You can learn more about the MCM competition here.

Twitter Feels

Twitter Feels

This website was developed by myself, Brennan McConnell, Seth Perry, and Denis Kazakov during Hack CU. The website allows users to query tweets with a certain keyword and perform sentiment analysis on it; the results from the query are then used to color in the states of the US where brighter colors represent a positive attitude and negative colors represent a negative attitude.

UIST Competition

UIST Student Competition

I competed in the UIST 2014 student competition along with Jackie Cameron and Derek Riemer. Our application utilized the Kinoma Create and several light sensors to allow visually impaired people the ability to tell whether different household devices were on based on the visual cues they had on them (e.g. an on light on a router). We were awarded 2nd place in the most useful category for our application.

Spectral Clustering

Spectral Clustering

For a our final project in our Matrix Methods class, Denis Kazakov, Rhys Olsen, and I learned about spectral clustering through several papers and articles. We then applied these concepts by using the Scipy package to analyze where bodies of water are on maps. A paper was also written to accompany these results.
